The Little Mermaid

The sculpture of The Little Mermaid is made of bronze and granite stone, inspired by H.C. Andersen's fairy tale about the mermaid who sacrifices everything to be united with the young, handsome prince on land. Every morning and evening, she rises from the depths of the sea, and from her rock in the water, she gazes longingly at the prince. The story of The Little Mermaid is known to many children today, not only from H.C. Andersen's original version but also from Disney's adaptation of the same name.

The Botanic Garden

The garden has been designed four times throughout history, with the establishment of the fourth and newest garden completed in 1874 during the transformation of the section of Østervold and the corresponding glacis from Gothersgade into the university's, now closed, observatory. The layout constitutes the current Botanical Garden, which was protected as a heritage site in 1969.

The Opera House

Experience the Opera's impressive foyer, which, with its overwhelming natural light, Olafur Eliasson's world-renowned light sculptures, and a unique view of Copenhagen Harbor and Frederiksstaden, embraces the glossy maple shell around the Main Stage. See the decorations by Per Kirkeby and Per Arnoldi, and hear the extraordinary story of the building's creation.

Den Lille Fede Restaurant

In the center of Copenhagen, right by Kongens Nytorv metro station, you'll find the restaurant "Den Lille Fede." A cozy and romantic restaurant with a focus on stimulating the senses. We emphasize providing good service but in a relaxed atmosphere. Therefore, you'll experience an informal tone and a playful twinkle in the eye when you visit us. We aim to serve a great dinner with a lovely glass of wine.
